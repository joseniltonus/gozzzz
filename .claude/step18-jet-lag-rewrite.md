### Updated Section: How Light and Melatonin Control Your Clock
**Key Principles (now in clear bullet points):**

1. **Morning light ADVANCES your clock** (fall asleep and wake earlier)
2. **Evening light DELAYS your clock** (fall asleep and wake later)
3. **Melatonin in early evening ADVANCES your clock** — timing is critical; incorrect timing has no effect or worsens jet lag

### Eastward Flights (you need to sleep earlier)
- Clear statement: you need to ADVANCE your clock
- Melatonin timing: 0.5-1mg in early evening (6pm-8pm) in home timezone, 3-4 days before travel
- Key guidance: Avoid light at the end of day when you arrive (would delay clock, worsening jet lag)
- Pre-travel: Sleep more the day before departure

### Westward Flights (you need to sleep later)
- Clear statement: you need to DELAY your clock
- Primary tool: Morning light in new timezone
- Avoid melatonin in first days (would advance clock even more)
- Caffeine in early morning in new location

### Universal Recovery Protocol
- Sunlight is most powerful synchronizer — maximum priority
- Meals at new schedule (fasting also synchronizes circadian clock)
- Avoid alcohol in first 2 days (depresses nervous system and impairs synchronization)
- Abundant hydration on flight

### Shift Workers
- Use bright light (10,000 lux) at start of work "day"
- Complete darkness at end
- Melatonin 30 minutes before sleep, regardless of clock time
